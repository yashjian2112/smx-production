4. Inventory Management

The Inventory tab is your main workspace. Here you manage all raw materials and stock.

Viewing Stock

  • See all materials with: name, code, category, current stock, minimum stock, unit of measure
  • Low stock items are highlighted (below minimum level)
  • Critical stock items are in red (near zero)
  • Search and filter by category or name

Stock Operations

Add Stock (Stock In)

  • Record incoming materials
  • Link to GRN/purchase order
  • Batch code auto-generated
  • Current stock increases

Issue Stock (Stock Out)

  • Dispatch materials to production
  • Link to job card/employee
  • Specify purpose and quantity
  • Current stock decreases

Adjust Stock

  • Correct stock after physical count
  • Record reason for adjustment
  • Creates audit trail

Reorder (Create RO)

  • Create requirement order for low items
  • Sent to Purchase Manager
  • Auto-calculated reorder quantity

5. Goods Receipt Note (GRN)

When materials arrive from a purchase order, you verify and record them:

  1. Go to the GRN tab
  2. Find the Goods Arrival Note (GAN) created by the Purchase Manager
  3. Physically verify the received materials against the PO
  4. Record received quantities for each item (may differ from ordered)
  5. Note any discrepancies or damage
  6. Submit the GRN — stock levels are automatically updated

6. Job Cards

When a production employee accepts an order, a Job Card is created. You are responsible for dispatching materials to them:

  1. Check the Job Cards tab for pending cards
  2. Each job card shows: employee name, order, product, stage, required materials
  3. Gather the required materials from inventory
  4. Mark the job card as DISPATCHED
  5. Stock is automatically deducted

7. Requirement Orders

View and create requirement orders for materials that need to be purchased:

  • Auto-generated ROs — triggered by low stock alerts or job card dispatches
  • Manual ROs — create when you know materials are needed in advance
  • Once an RO is approved, the Purchase Manager creates an RFQ for vendors
Tip: Keep minimum stock levels updated for each material. This ensures automatic ROs are triggered at the right time, preventing production delays.
Important: Every stock movement (in, out, adjustment) is recorded permanently in the system for audit purposes. Always enter accurate quantities.
