PRODUCTION EMPLOYEE

1. Overview

As a Production Employee, you work on manufacturing stages — building powerstages, brainboards, assembling controllers, running QC checks, and completing final assembly. The app tracks your work, shows your tasks, and records your output.

2. Getting Started

Login

Open the app in your browser. Enter your email and password, then click Sign in.

If face verification is enabled, you'll need to verify your face before starting work each shift (lasts 8 hours).

4. Accepting Work (Orders Tab)

Before you can start building, you need to accept an order. This is how you get assigned work:

  1. Go to the Orders tab
  2. Look at the Pending tab — these are orders needing workers
  3. Each order card shows: product name, stage needed, pending unit count, due date
  4. Click Accept Order on an order you want to work on
  5. A Job Card is created for you — the store team will dispatch materials
  6. Once materials are dispatched, your job card status changes to DISPATCHED and you can start
Tip: You can only work on one unit at a time per stage. Finish your current unit before picking up the next one.

5. Working on a Unit (The Main Workflow)

This is your primary daily workflow — scanning a barcode and submitting your work:

  1. Scan the barcode — use the barcode scanner on the unit's label. The app identifies the unit and loads the correct stage.
  2. Start work — click the Start button. Your timer begins and the unit moves to IN_PROGRESS.
  3. Complete the checklist — follow the quality checklist items shown on screen. Check off each item as you complete it.
  4. Take photos — photograph the completed work as required (e.g., PCB board photo for AI quality check).
  5. Submit work — click Submit. Your work is recorded and the unit moves to COMPLETED or WAITING_APPROVAL.
  6. Wait for approval — the Production Manager reviews your work. If approved, the unit advances to the next stage. If rejected, you'll see it back in your tasks with feedback.

6. My Tasks

The My Tasks tab shows all your currently active work:

  • Units you've started but not yet submitted
  • Units rejected back to you by the PM (with feedback)
  • Filter by stage to focus on one type of work
  • Click any unit to continue working on it
Important: If your work is rejected, read the PM's feedback carefully before re-doing it. The feedback appears on the unit detail page.

7. Barcode Types

Each stage has its own barcode format. Here's how to identify which stage a barcode belongs to:

  • PS in the code = Powerstage (e.g., C100PS2501)
  • BB in the code = Brainboard (e.g., C100BB2501)
  • AY in the code = Assembly (e.g., C100AY2501)
  • QC in the code = QC & Software (e.g., C100QC2501)
  • No stage prefix = Final Assembly (e.g., C10025A001)

8. My Performance

Track your productivity from the My Performance page:

  • Units completed per day/week
  • Average build time per stage
  • Approval rate (how often your work passes first time)
Tip: Aim for a high first-pass approval rate — this means fewer rework cycles and faster production.
